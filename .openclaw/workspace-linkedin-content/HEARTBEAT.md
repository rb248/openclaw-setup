- [ ] Weekend review: Any recruiter DMs to respond to?
- [ ] Content calendar check: What's the week's focus?

### Mid-Week Check (Wednesday)
- [ ] Posting streak on track? (2-3 posts so far)
- [ ] Comments made: 2-3 so far this week?
- [ ] Any new target company engagement?
- [ ] Post #3 of the week published (Wednesday post)

### Friday Check
- [ ] Weekly goals met?
  - Posts: ___/3 (or ___/5)
  - Comments: ___/5
  - New target employees engaged: ___
- [ ] Recruiter DMs received this week: ___
- [ ] Plan next week's content (20 interview questions list)

### Monthly Review (Last Friday of Month)
- [ ] Review posted content: What resonated?
- [ ] Check recruiter/industry interactions

## Quiet Hiring Reminder

**The current job market:**
- Recruiters are NOT posting the best jobs publicly
- They're using LinkedIn's AI to find candidates proactively
- Your content = your 24/7 interview
- Activity signals = higher visibility in search

**This week's focus:**
- Post consistently about your 2-3 pain pillars
- Comment thoughtfully on target company posts
- Build your "career asset library"

**The goal:** Get recruiter DMs saying "You're a match, you're active. Want to chat?"

## Success Indicators to Watch

**Good signs:**
- Profile views from target companies
- Connection requests from industry professionals
- Comments on your posts from target company employees
- Recruiter DMs in inbox

**Red flags:**
- No activity for 3+ days
- Posting random topics outside pain pillars
- Only posting, never commenting
- Generic "congrats" comments only

## Emergency Actions

**If no posting for 3+ days:**
1. Post today (use Interview Answer template)
2. Don't overthink it - post imperfectly
3. Get back on the streak

**If running out of content ideas:**
1. Pick from the 20 interview questions list
2. Share a learning from a recent article/course
3. Tell a project story (what worked, what didn't)

**If feeling discouraged:**
1. Remember: metrics don't matter, right people finding you does
2. Review the 17-day success story
3. You're building a career asset library, not chasing likes
4. 15 mins/day is enough
